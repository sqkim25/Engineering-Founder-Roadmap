Layer 1: Mathematical Foundations

Calculus, Algebra, Probability – building the first-principles toolbox.

Learning Objectives

Develop rigorous mathematical thinking and problem-solving skills. Master calculus (differentiation, integration, series), linear algebra, and probability to provide a toolbox for modeling physical systems and algorithms. Build the “first principles” mindset to reason quantitatively about engineering problems (as Elon Musk did by studying textbooks to self-educate in rocket science).

Subskills

Core topics to master:

  • Calculus (single-variable & multivariable)
  • Differential equations
  • Linear algebra (matrix operations, eigenvalues)
  • Discrete mathematics (logic, combinatorics)
  • Probability and statistics

Real-World Examples

SpaceX: Rocket trajectory calculation requires solving differential equations for motion and orbital mechanics.

Tesla: Battery performance modeling uses calculus and statistics to predict range under various conditions.

OpenAI: Linear algebra underpins neural network operations (matrix multiplications in GPUs).

Strong math skills are fundamental, “invisible” enablers of advanced engineering – you only notice their absence when problems arise.

Evaluation Metrics

  • Solve problem sets and exams from MIT-level math courses (e.g., evaluate integrals, solve ODEs) with full solutions.
  • Improve speed and accuracy in solving engineering word problems.
  • Compete in math contests or perform well on online judges for math-oriented problems.
  • Periodically take a math diagnostic (SAT Math Level II or MIT Advanced Standing Exams) to ensure retention.
  • Mastery is reached when you can apply math to new problems effortlessly – for example, deriving the equations for a drone’s flight dynamics from scratch.
